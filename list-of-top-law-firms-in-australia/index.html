What is a Law Firm?

A law firm is a business entity formed by one or more lawyers to engage in the practice of law. Law firms provide legal services to individuals, businesses, and government agencies in various legal matters.

The primary function of a law firm is to advise clients on their legal rights and responsibilities and represent them in legal disputes. Law firms can specialize in various areas of law, such as corporate law, intellectual property law, family law, and criminal law, among others.

The history of law firms in Australia dates back to the early 1800s when the first law firms were established in the country. Over the years, the legal industry has grown significantly, and today, there are countless law firms operating in the country.
