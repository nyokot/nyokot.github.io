When it comes to starting a business, franchising is a popular option for many entrepreneurs. But how does franchising work in the Philippines? In this article, we will dive into the ins and outs of franchising in the country, including its history, myths, and hidden secrets.

The Pain Points of Franchising in the Philippines

Many aspiring business owners in the Philippines face challenges when it comes to starting a business from scratch. Some of these challenges include lack of capital, lack of experience, and high levels of competition. Franchising offers a solution to these problems by providing a proven business model, brand recognition, and ongoing support from the franchisor. However, the cost of franchising can be high, and there is a risk of failure if the business is not run properly.

How Does Franchising Work in the Philippines?

Franchising in the Philippines works similarly to franchising in other countries. The franchisor grants the franchisee the right to use their business model, brand, and products/services in exchange for a fee and ongoing royalties. The franchisee is responsible for running the business and adhering to the franchisor's guidelines and standards. The franchisor provides support in areas such as training, marketing, and operations.

What is Franchising?

Franchising is a business model in which a franchisor grants the right to use their business model, brand, and products/services to a franchisee in exchange for a fee and ongoing royalties. This model allows entrepreneurs to start a business with the support of an established brand and system.

The History and Myth of Franchising in the Philippines

Franchising in the Philippines has been around since the 1970s, with the first international franchise being McDonald's in 1981. However, there are also myths surrounding franchising, such as the idea that it is a "get rich quick" scheme or that franchisees have no control over their business. These myths can be dispelled by understanding the realities of franchising and working with a reputable franchisor.

The Hidden Secrets of Franchising in the Philippines

One of the hidden secrets of franchising in the Philippines is that it can be a lucrative business for both the franchisor and the franchisee. However, success requires hard work, dedication, and a willingness to learn. Another secret is that not all franchises are created equal, and it is important to do your research before investing in a franchise.

Recommendations for Franchising in the Philippines

If you are considering franchising in the Philippines, it is important to choose a reputable franchisor that aligns with your values and goals. What are the Costs of Franchising in the Philippines?

The costs of franchising in the Philippines can vary widely depending on the franchisor and the type of business. Some franchise fees can be as low as Php 50,000, while others can be upwards of Php 5 million. In addition to the franchise fee, franchisees must also pay ongoing royalties and other fees.

Fun Facts about Franchising in the Philippines

How to Franchise in the Philippines

To franchise in the Philippines, you must first identify a franchisor that aligns with your values and goals. You should then submit a franchise application and go through a screening process. If you are approved, you will sign a franchise agreement and pay the franchise fee. You will then receive training and support from the franchisor to help you set up and run your business.

What If Franchising Doesn't Work?

If franchising doesn't work out, there are other options for starting a business in the Philippines. These include starting a business from scratch, buying an existing business, or investing in a different type of business model such as direct selling or e-commerce.
