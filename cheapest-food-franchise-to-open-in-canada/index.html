Cheapest Food Franchise to Open in Canada: A Targeted Answer

The cheapest food franchise to open in Canada is Subway. With an initial investment ranging from $116,000 to $263,000, it's a relatively low-cost option compared to other food franchises.

Subway has been around since 1965 and has established itself as a reputable brand worldwide. It's known for its customizable sandwiches, fresh ingredients, and healthy options. The franchise also offers ongoing support for its franchisees, making it a great option for first-time business owners.

Cheapest Food Franchise to Open in Canada: What Is It?

Subway is a fast-food franchise that specializes in customizable sandwiches made with fresh ingredients. The company has over 41,000 locations worldwide and has been in business since 1965.

Cheapest Food Franchise to Open in Canada: History and Myth

Subway was founded in Bridgeport, Connecticut, in 1965 by Fred DeLuca and Peter Buck. The company's name was originally Pete's Super Submarines but was changed to Subway in 1968. The franchise quickly grew in popularity and expanded to Canada in the early 1980s.

One common myth about Subway is that its bread contains a chemical called azodicarbonamide, which is also used in yoga mats. However, this is not true, and Subway removed the ingredient from its bread in 2014.

Cheapest Food Franchise to Open in Canada: More Details

In addition to the initial investment, owning a Subway franchise requires a 12.5% royalty fee and a 4.5% advertising fee. The franchise agreement is for 20 years, with the option to renew for an additional 20 years.

As a franchise owner, you'll be responsible for managing the day-to-day operations of the restaurant, including finances, staffing, and inventory management. However, with the support provided by Subway, you'll have the resources and guidance to run a successful business.
