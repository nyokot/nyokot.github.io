Do I Have to Pay Franchise Tax in Texas?

Running a business in Texas can be challenging, with numerous regulations to follow, including paying franchise tax. Many business owners are unsure whether they need to pay franchise tax or not, leading to confusion and stress. In this article, we will explore everything you need to know about franchise tax in Texas, so you can run your business with confidence.

Franchise tax is a state tax that businesses in Texas must pay. It is based on the taxable margin of the business, which is calculated using a specific formula. The tax applies to all types of businesses, from corporations to partnerships and LLCs. Failure to pay franchise tax can lead to penalties, interest, and even the revocation of your business's charter.

If you own a business in Texas, the answer is likely yes - you do need to pay franchise tax. However, there are some exceptions, such as businesses with no revenue or those that qualify for certain exemptions. To determine if you need to pay franchise tax, you can use the Comptroller's Public Information Database or consult with a tax professional.
