What is Subway?

Subway is a fast food chain that specializes in sandwiches and salads. Founded in 1965, Subway has grown to become one of the largest fast food franchises in the world, with over 40,000 locations in more than 100 countries.

History and Myth of Subway

Subway was founded by Fred DeLuca and Peter Buck in 1965. The first Subway location was in Bridgeport, Connecticut, and it was called "Pete's Super Submarines." The name was later changed to Subway, and the rest is history.

One myth about Subway is that the company got its name from its founder, Fred DeLuca, who was a submariner in the U.S. Navy. However, this is not true, as DeLuca was never a submariner. The name "Subway" was chosen because it represented the idea of a fast, efficient mode of transportation.
