One of the biggest pain points when it comes to buying a business name in Canada is the paperwork involved. It can be overwhelming, especially for first-time business owners, to figure out what forms need to be filled out and how to navigate the process.

To buy a business name in Canada, you'll need to start by choosing a name that is unique and not already registered by another business. Then, you'll need to conduct a NUANS search, which is a database of all existing business names in Canada. Once you've confirmed that your chosen name is available, you'll need to register it with the government.

In summary, the main steps involved in buying a business name in Canada are: choosing a unique name, conducting a NUANS search, and registering the name with the government.
