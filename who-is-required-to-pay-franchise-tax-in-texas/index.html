What is franchise tax in Texas?

Franchise tax is a tax that businesses operating in Texas are required to pay. The tax is calculated based on the business's taxable margin, which is determined by subtracting the cost of goods sold from its total revenue. The tax rate varies depending on the type of business and its annual revenue.

The History and Myth of Franchise Tax in Texas

Franchise tax has been in place in Texas since 1943, and it was initially called the "privilege tax." The tax was created to offset revenue losses from the elimination of the corporate income tax. Over the years, the tax has undergone several changes, with the most recent being the reduction of the tax rate in 2019.
