What is Franchise Business for Sale in New Zealand?

A franchise business for sale in New Zealand is a business model where an individual (the franchisee) purchases the right to use an established brand, products, and services from a franchisor. The franchisor provides ongoing training, support, and marketing resources to the franchisee. In exchange, the franchisee pays an initial franchise fee and ongoing royalties to the franchisor.

History and Myth of Franchise Business for Sale in New Zealand

Franchising has been around for centuries, but it wasn't until the 20th century that it became a popular business model. The first modern franchise was developed by Isaac Singer, who used franchising to distribute sewing machines in the 1850s. However, it wasn't until the 1950s and 1960s that franchising really took off in the United States.

One common myth about franchising is that it's a "get rich quick" scheme. While owning a franchise can be a lucrative business opportunity, it's important to note that it still requires hard work and dedication. Franchisees need to follow the franchisor's established business model, and they still need to put in the effort to build and grow their business.
