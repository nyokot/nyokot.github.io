What is Franchising?

Franchising is a business model that allows entrepreneurs to use an already established brand and operating system to start their business. The franchisor provides the franchisee with the right to use their brand and operating system in exchange for a fee or royalty. The franchisee operates their business under the franchisor's brand and follows their operating system.

History and Myth of Franchising

Franchising has a long history dating back to the 19th century. The concept of franchising was first used by the Singer Sewing Machine Company, which allowed entrepreneurs to sell their sewing machines under their brand in exchange for a fee. Since then, franchising has grown in popularity and is now used in many different industries.

One myth about franchising is that it is only for large corporations. In reality, there are many small franchise opportunities available for entrepreneurs. Franchising offers a way for entrepreneurs to start a business without the risks associated with starting a new venture.

How to Franchise

If you are interested in franchising your business, there are several steps you need to take. First, you need to have a proven business model and an established brand.
