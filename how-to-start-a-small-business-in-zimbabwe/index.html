One of the biggest pain points when it comes to starting a small business in Zimbabwe is the lack of access to funding. Banks and other financial institutions are often hesitant to lend money to small businesses, making it difficult for entrepreneurs to get off the ground. Additionally, navigating the legal and regulatory landscape can be a challenge, with many entrepreneurs struggling to understand the rules and regulations surrounding business ownership and operation.

Despite these challenges, there are many ways to successfully start a small business in Zimbabwe. The first step is to identify a viable business idea and conduct thorough market research to determine if there is demand for your product or service. From there, you will need to create a business plan, register your business, and secure any necessary licenses and permits.
