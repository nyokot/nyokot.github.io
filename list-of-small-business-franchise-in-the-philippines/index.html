What is a Small Business Franchise?

A small business franchise is a business model in which an entrepreneur purchases the right to operate a business under an established brand. The franchisee pays a fee to the franchisor in exchange for the right to use the brand name, products or services, and business model. The franchisor provides support and training to the franchisee to ensure the success of the business.

History and Myth of Small Business Franchises in the Philippines

The concept of franchising first gained popularity in the Philippines in the 1980s. As the country's economy grew, more people were looking for opportunities to start their own businesses. Franchising provided a way for aspiring entrepreneurs to start a business with a proven model and a recognizable brand.

One common myth about franchising is that it's only for big businesses. In reality, there are many small business franchises available in the Philippines that are affordable and profitable.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the average franchise fee for a small business franchise in the Philippines?

A: The average franchise fee for a small business franchise in the Philippines is around P100,000 to P200,000.

Q: Do I need prior business experience to start a small business franchise?

A: No, you don't need prior business experience to start a small business franchise. The franchisor provides training and support to ensure your success.

Q: How much can I expect to earn from a small business franchise?

A: Earnings from a small business franchise vary depending on the franchise, location, and other factors. However, most franchises offer high profit margins, making it a profitable investment.

Q: Can I own multiple small business franchises?

A: Yes, you can own multiple small business franchises. However, it's important to ensure that you have the resources and capacity to manage multiple businesses.
