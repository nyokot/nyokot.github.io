What are franchises?

Franchises are a type of business model in which an individual (the franchisee) pays a fee to use the name, logo, and business model of an established brand (the franchisor). In return, the franchisee receives training, support, and access to the franchisor's resources and network. Franchises can be found in a variety of industries, from food and beverage to retail and services.

History and Myth of Franchises

The first franchise is said to have been started by Isaac Singer, the founder of Singer Sewing Machines, in the mid-1800s. Singer allowed entrepreneurs to sell his machines in exchange for a percentage of their profits. This model proved successful and was soon adopted by others, leading to the franchising industry we know today.

The Hidden Secret of Franchises

One of the hidden secrets of franchises is the level of support and resources provided by the franchisor. When you invest in a franchise, you're not just buying a name and logo; you're buying into a system that has been proven to work. Franchisors provide ongoing training, marketing support, and access to a network of other franchisees who can offer advice and support.

Q: What is the average initial investment for a franchise?

Q: Do I need to have previous business experience to buy a franchise?
